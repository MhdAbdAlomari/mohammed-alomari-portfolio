## The cost is real

Three layers, repositories, mappers, use-cases. On day one of Ashafaq, this felt like overhead. On day ninety, when payments changed providers, it felt like the smartest thing I'd done.

## Where it pays off

Anywhere external systems can change: payments, notifications, persistence. The interface boundary means swapping the implementation doesn't touch your UI.

Testing is the other big win. Use-cases are pure functions over the domain; they don't care about Flutter at all.

## Where I bend the rules

Trivial features don't need three layers. I keep the layered approach for anything touching external systems, and let small UI-only features be small.
